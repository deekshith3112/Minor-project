Historical Places in India

Hampi

Hampi is an ancient village and UNESCO World Heritage Site in the state of Karnataka, India. It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort located in Delhi, India. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a minaret located in Delhi, India. It is the tallest minaret in the world made of bricks and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace is a historical palace located in the city of Mysore, Karnataka, India. It is one of the most famous tourist attractions in the country.

Fatehpur Sikri

fatehpur sikri is a historical city located in the agra district of the indian state of uttar pradesh it was founded by the mughal emperor akbar in the late 16th century.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is a mausoleum located in Delhi,India,and is one of the finest examples of mughal architecture.It was built in mid-16th century by Haji Begum.

Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th century hindu temple located in the town of Konark in the indian state of odisha.

Charminar

The Charminar is a monument located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. Constructed in 1591, the landmark is a symbol of Hyderabad and officially incorporated in the emblem of Telangana

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located near the Kartavya path on the eastern edge of the "ceremonial axis" of New Delhi, formerly called duty path.

Mahabalipuram

Mamallapuram, also known as Mahabalipuram, is a town in Chengalpattu district in the southeastern Indian state of Tamil Nadu, best known for the UNESCO World Heritage Site of 7th- and 8th-century Hindu Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram.
